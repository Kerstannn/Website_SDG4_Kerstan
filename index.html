About

The Climate Action Hackathon 2025 Urban Heat & Flood Resilience is a thematic innovation event focused on addressing the growing climate challenges in urban areas, especially rising temperatures and frequent flash floods.

The hackathon brings together students, developers, designers, and community innovators to co-create digital solutions that support SDG 13: Climate Action. Participants will work in teams to build prototypes, apps, or visualization tools that help communities:

  • Mitigate urban heat through green infrastructure mapping, smart shading, or cooling strategies.
  • Improve flood resilience by creating early-warning systems, community response tools, or interactive dashboards.
  • Raise climate risk awareness using data visualization, storytelling, or geospatial tools.

During the hackathon, we are expecting specific outcomes by the end of this event such as:

  • Innovative Prototypes and Solutions
  • Cross-Sector Collaboration and Partnerships
  • Public Awareness and Education Campaigns
  • Validation and Testing of Concepts
  • Increased Capacity for Urban Resilience

SDG 13 Connection

In this Hackathon, our proposed solutions aim to address the urban heat and flooding problems we are currently facing. Our solutions will be beneficial for everyone as we will develop plans to mitigate these problems such as implementing green structures, using nature-based solutions, developing plans to reduce energy consumption, climate risk awareness, early-warning systems, etc.

Tracks & Challenges

Heat Mitigation

Since reducing heat in cities involve big and ambitious ideas, costs would prove to be a challenge as it can end up being very costly. Additionally, urban areas often trap heat due to concrete surfaces, lack of greenery, and high energy use, making it difficult to design solutions that work for different climates and communities.

Flood Early Warning

This requires accurate data and reliable technology to work. This is a challenge because we do not possess such equipment. Furthermore, participants would need to create solutions that are low-cost, easy to use, and adaptable to different local conditions within a limited timeframe.

Green Infrastructure

Green infrastructures, while a great idea, can be difficult because integrating natural systems like parks, green roofs, and rain gardens into urban environments that are already densely built. Designing solutions that balance space limitations, cost, and maintenance while still providing cooling and flood control benefits can be complex.

Community Apps

Designing an app that is easy to use, accessible to all ages and backgrounds, and also motivating communities to respond to heat or flood risks can be difficult.
